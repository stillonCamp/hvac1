IAQ

Indoor air quality (IAQ) refers to the quality of the air inside a building and its impact on the health, comfort, and well-being of the occupants. Poor IAQ can lead to sick building syndrome, reduced productivity, and health problems. Common indoor air pollutants include secondhand smoke, radon, carbon monoxide, and volatile organic compounds. IAQ can be improved through source control, filtration, and ventilation to dilute contaminants.

Ventilation

Ductwork is a system of ducts, typically made of steel, aluminum, polyurethane, or fiberglass, used to circulate air for heating, cooling, or ventilation in a building. It acts as a conduit to distribute heated or cooled air from the HVAC unit to different rooms and then recirculate it back through the system. Properly designed and maintained ductwork is essential for efficient HVAC performance.

Units

The systems that control the temperature, humidity, and air quality in an enclosed space. It encompasses various technologies and components to work properly. A split system consists of both indoor and outdoor units connected by copper tubing. The outdoor unit contains the compressor and condenser, while the indoor unit contains the evaporator coil and air handling unit.This setup allows for more customization and can include heat pumps, air conditioners, gas furnaces, or fan coils.
